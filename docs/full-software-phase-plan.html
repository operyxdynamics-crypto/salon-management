Delivery phases
Full software goal: a multi-tenant salon SaaS where a salon can onboard, get approved, manage branches, staff, customers, appointments, POS, invoices, inventory, benefits, attendance, payroll summaries, reports, and later publish its own salon website and marketplace profile.
₹49,000
Already invested in product planning, prototype, database-backed salon panel, admin, POS, invoices and operating modules.
₹50,000
Additional amount required to make the MVP live-ready with final QA, deployment, hardening and launch support.
₹99,000
Total planned MVP investment before public pilot launch, excluding future paid ads, legal, trademark and large-scale support hiring.

Introductory MVP offer

L
Founding Salon Offer
First 10-20 salons
  • Offer first month free only after setup fee is collected, so the launch does not become cash-negative.
  • Give discounted annual plan: pay for 10 months and get 12 months access.
  • Keep pricing simple: no online payment processing in MVP, only offline payment recording.
  • Collect feedback weekly from pilot salons and convert useful requests into paid roadmap items.
S
Sales Focus
Fastest path to revenue
  • Start with premium salons, bridal studios and salons already struggling with billing, appointments and staff tracking.
  • Sell the complete daily flow: appointment, POS, invoice, stock, rewards, register close and reports.
  • Use Operyx as the live demo brand and keep one clean demo workspace ready.
  • Avoid custom development for the first clients unless it is paid separately.

Revenue recovery strategy

1
Recover from setup fees first
Immediate cash flow

Do not make every pilot free. Charge a small setup/training fee so implementation effort is covered and salons take onboarding seriously.

2
Push annual plans
Cash upfront

Offer 2 months free on annual billing. Ten Growth salons paying annually can create strong upfront cash flow.

3
Keep MVP scope controlled
Protect margin

Marketplace enhancements, website builder, custom domains and online payments should come after salon operations are stable and subscription revenue starts.

4
Measure payback monthly
Business discipline

Track MRR, setup revenue, server cost, support cost, churn and feature requests every month. The target is to recover ₹99,000 before major future-module spending.

Counter flow for salon staff

R
Reception appointment flow
Fast booking for existing and new customers
  • Select branch if owner is viewing all branches.
  • Search customer by name or mobile number.
  • Add new customer if no match, then return to saved booking draft.
  • Select one or more services from category-grouped service master.
  • Select date, available slot and qualified staff.
  • Create appointment with conflict checks and idempotency key.
  • Appointment appears in smart list, calendar and dashboard immediately.
P
POS and invoice flow
Money and benefits loop
  • Select customer or use Create sale from an appointment, which preloads branch, customer, appointment, services and staff attribution.
  • Add services and products with staff attribution.
  • Preview wallet, loyalty, package and gift card balances.
  • Apply discounts, redemptions, tips and payment split.
  • Generate GST or non-GST invoice with correct sequence.
  • Deduct stock, calculate commissions and update benefits atomically.
  • Open invoice detail for print or browser save-as-PDF.

Screen design requirements

1
Workspace shell
Navigation and account area
  • Clear active menu state and no overlapping user/logout card.
  • Fixed branch selector and quick actions.
  • Desktop sidebar for large screens; mobile app shell with bottom tabs and More sheet.
  • Role-aware hidden or disabled navigation items.
2
Dashboard
Daily command center
  • Top quick actions: New appointment, New sale, Add customer, Add expense, Stock entry.
  • KPI row: revenue, appointments, customers, average ticket, outstanding, staff present.
  • Middle widgets: calendar preview, source mix, payment split, low stock, recent invoices.
  • Every metric clickable to filtered report.
3
Appointments
List-first scheduling
  • Mobile default is the appointment queue/list: who is coming now, what is next, and what action is needed.
  • Calendar remains available as a secondary visual view for desktop/tablet scheduling.
  • Click appointment to open detail drawer or full-screen mobile sheet.
  • Drag-and-drop and resize remain desktop/tablet actions with confirmation and conflict recheck.
4
POS
Counter sale speed
  • Mobile POS opens directly to current sale with customer, cart total and payment CTA visible.
  • Sections: customer, linked appointment, cart, redemptions, payment, summary.
  • Customer balance preview for wallet, rewards, gift cards and packages.
  • Open created invoice automatically after checkout.
5
Tables and lists
Readable operational data
  • Sticky headers, status chips, row hover and quick filters on desktop.
  • Mobile card layout for appointment, invoice, customer, inventory and staff rows.
  • Open action visible on clickable records.
  • CSV export where business users need reconciliation.
6
Forms and drawers
Clear input and validation
  • Every input needs a visible label and useful placeholder.
  • Required fields and validation errors should be explicit.
  • Primary and secondary actions should be visually distinct.
  • Drawers for appointment, invoice, customer, service, staff and product details.
